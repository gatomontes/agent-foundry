# Staffing Pattern — Small Strike Team

## Purpose

This staffing pattern defines a minimal operational formation capable of solving focused objectives with coherent verification and low orchestration overhead.

The objective is efficient capability concentration.

---

# Recommended Use Cases

Examples:

- focused implementation
- rapid architecture iteration
- contained research tasks
- operational debugging
- proof-of-concept development
- lightweight governance review

---

# Team Composition

## Minimal Formation

- Systems Architect
- Runtime Operator
- Verification Specialist

## Optional Additions

Add only when justified:

- Critique Authority
- Auditor
- Executive Secretary

---

# Operational Characteristics

The Small Strike Team favors:

- low coordination overhead
- rapid iteration
- coherent ownership
- contained dependency chains
- fast escalation visibility

---

# Ownership Model

Every active workstream should define:

- owner
- expected artifact
- verification path
- completion signal

Avoid overlapping ownership.

---

# Verification Model

Verification remains mandatory.

However:

- audit may remain optional
- orchestration layers remain lightweight
- staffing inflation should be resisted

---

# Escalation Conditions

Escalate when:

- consequence tier increases
- dependency density expands
- verification becomes insufficient
- architectural redesign emerges
- operational coordination pressure rises

At escalation threshold, transition into larger staffing topology.

---

# Common Failure Modes

- role overlap
- hidden orchestration complexity
- verification shortcuts
- premature scaling
- overdelegation

---

# Constitutional Principle

Operational elegance favors coherent minimality over decorative organizational size.
