# Mission Blueprint — Research Operation

## Purpose

This blueprint defines a reusable institutional structure for conducting research-oriented operations inside The Foundry.

The objective is not merely information gathering.

The objective is:

- evidence acquisition
- synthesis
- contradiction exposure
- operational contextualization
- trustworthy reporting

---

# Typical Objectives

Examples:

- technology evaluation
- architecture comparison
- market research
- competitor analysis
- operational feasibility analysis
- doctrine review
- security intelligence gathering

---

# Recommended Profession Topology

## Core Team

- Research Analyst
- Systems Architect
- Verification Specialist

## Expanded Team

Add when consequence or complexity increases:

- Auditor
- Critique Authority
- Executive Secretary

---

# Staffing Pattern

```txt
Executive Intake
  -> Research Analyst
    -> Systems Architect
      -> Verification Specialist
        -> Critique Authority
          -> Auditor
```

---

# Artifact Expectations

Typical artifacts:

- research brief
- evidence summary
- contradiction report
- comparative matrix
- architecture assessment
- verification report
- critique findings

---

# Verification Expectations

Research operations should preserve:

- evidence traceability
- citation lineage
- contradiction visibility
- confidence estimation
- uncertainty exposure

Important or critical research should favor independent verification.

---

# Escalation Conditions

Escalation recommended when:

- evidence conflicts significantly
- consequence tier increases
- governance implications emerge
- architectural redesign becomes necessary
- uncertainty remains unresolved

---

# Common Failure Modes

- confirmation bias
- citation laundering
- overconfident synthesis
- missing contradictory evidence
- research without operational relevance
- weak verification lineage

---

# Constitutional Principle

Research exists to reduce uncertainty honestly, not to manufacture certainty aesthetically.
