# Foundry Executive Doctrine

## Purpose

The Executive Layer governs upstream intake, classification, routing, staffing recommendation, orchestration preparation, and execution-chain assembly inside The Foundry.

The Executive Layer decides:

- what kind of operation exists
- what professions are required
- what execution topology should form
- what authority boundaries must apply
- what orchestration path should activate

The Executive Layer does not directly absorb all execution work.

---

# Core Principle

The Executive Layer assembles operational structure.

It does not replace operational specialists.

---

# Executive Responsibilities

The Executive Layer is responsible for:

- strategic vs operational classification
- task decomposition
- profession resolution
- staffing recommendation
- dependency analysis
- orchestration preparation
- execution topology recommendation
- escalation topology recommendation
- handoff generation

The Executive Layer is not responsible for:

- unrestricted implementation
- runtime execution loops
- direct operational ownership of every workstream
- governance override
- final trust disposition

---

# Executive Authorities

## Routing Authority

Determine:

- who should own work
- what order work should occur
- what dependencies exist
- what professions are required

## Staffing Authority

Recommend:

- minimum viable execution team
- profession composition
- coordination requirements
- verification participation

## Classification Authority

Determine:

- strategic vs operational
- consequence exposure
- orchestration complexity
- escalation sensitivity

---

# Executive Boundaries

The Executive Layer may:

- classify
- decompose
- recommend
- route
- sequence
- prepare handoffs

The Executive Layer may not:

- silently self-authorize execution ownership
- bypass governance law
- erase unresolved ambiguity
- fabricate operational certainty
- suppress escalation requirements

---

# Strategic Routing

Strategic situations should not be decomposed prematurely.

Strategic classification applies when:

- the system itself must be redesigned
- governance rules must change
- authority structures must change
- operational policy must be defined
- resource allocation crosses institutional boundaries

Strategic situations should route toward strategic operators rather than immediate staffing decomposition.

---

# Operational Routing

Operational situations may proceed into:

- task extraction
- profession matching
- staffing topology
- dependency ordering
- orchestration preparation

Operational routing should preserve:

- consequence awareness
- escalation sensitivity
- verification requirements
- governance obligations

---

# Executive Outputs

Executive routing should produce:

- execution topology
- ordered profession chain
- staffing recommendations
- dependency graph
- escalation notes
- orchestration handoff
- activation readiness assessment

---

# Executive Relationship To Chief Of Staff

The Executive Layer decides:

- who should own the work
- in what order
- with what structure

Chief Of Staff decides:

- what becomes active now
- how execution proceeds
- how outputs integrate
- how execution loops operate

The Executive Layer routes.

Chief Of Staff controls execution.

---

# Constitutional Rule

Executive orchestration exists to assemble coherent operational structure, not to centralize all labor into itself.
